**Starter resume/cover‑letter build rules (please review and edit as needed)**  

**Length & Brevity**  
- Keep the resume to 2 pages maximum; aim for concise bullet points (3–5 per role).  
- Cover letter: 1 page, 3–4 short paragraphs.  

**Section Order (recommended)**  
1. Header (Name, title, contact links)  
2. Professional Summary (1‑2 sentences)  
3. Core Competencies / Technical Skills (inline list, top 6‑8)  
4. Professional Experience (most recent first)  
5. Selected Projects & Achievements (highlight AI/AI‑product impact)  
6. Education  
7. Certifications  
8. Languages  
9. Honors & Awards  

**Tone & Voice**  
- Use active verbs, quantifiable outcomes where available.  
- Maintain a confident, professional tone; avoid overly casual language.  

**ATS‑Friendliness**  
- Use standard headings (e.g., “Professional Experience”, “Education”).  
- Avoid tables, graphics, or complex markdown that may be stripped by ATS.  
- Include keywords from target job descriptions (e.g., “Full‑Stack”, “AI/ML”, “Generative AI”, “TypeScript”, “Angular”, “Cloud”, “Microservices”).  

**Styling Guidelines**  
- Plain text or simple markdown; bold only for company names/role titles.  
- Dates formatted as “Month Year – Month Year” or “Month Year – Present”.  
- Consistent indentation: role title on one line, company name bolded, location optional, then bullet list.  
- Use bullet points (‑) not numbered lists for responsibilities.  

**Customization Tips**  
- Tailor the “Professional Summary” and “Core Competencies” to match each posting’s top requirements.  
- Highlight industry‑relevant experience (Fintech for PayPal, Media for Meta, etc.) in the cover letter opening.  
- Add a brief “Why I’m a fit” paragraph linking personal mission with the company’s vision.  

**Final Checklist**  
- ✅ All dates are chronologically accurate and non‑overlapping.  
- ✅ No invented metrics; use only figures present in the source résumé.  
- ✅ Contact information includes at least one professional email (add if available).  
- ✅ Proofread for spelling, grammar, and consistent punctuation.  

*These rules provide a solid foundation; adjust tone, length, and focus to suit specific roles and company cultures.*
